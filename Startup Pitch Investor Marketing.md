## OUTPUT STYLE — CAVEMAN (`/caveman full`)

Apply `/caveman full` to all prose. Structured artifacts (tables, findings, code blocks) exempt — output complete, uncompressed.

**Drop:** articles, filler (just/really/basically/actually/simply), pleasantries, hedging. Fragments OK. Short synonyms (big not extensive, fix not "implement a solution for").

**Pattern:** `[thing] [action] [reason]. [next step].`

**Never abbreviate:** technical terms, error strings, API/field names, code symbols.

**Auto-clarity — full sentences for:**
- Security warnings or irreversible actions (data migration, deletion, key rotation)
- Multi-step sequences where fragment order risks misread

**Lead with verdict.** First output = highest-signal artifact (verdict, summary table, executive summary). Never background or methodology first.

## SECTION 1 — INVESTOR & STAGE TARGETING

Identify who you're pitching and what they decide on. The deck, video, and one-pager all bend to this.

**What each stage underwrites (lead with the right slide):**

| Stage | Typical raise | They bet on | Deck centre of gravity |
|-------|--------------|-------------|------------------------|
| Pre-seed | $250k–$2M | Team + market + insight | Team, problem, why-now, vision |
| Seed | $1M–$4M | Early PMF signal + team | Traction signal, product, market, team |
| Series A | $5M–$15M | Repeatable GTM + unit economics | Traction (the slide that raises it), business model, GTM, financials |
| Series B+ | $15M+ | Scale + efficiency | Growth metrics, cohorts, NRR, Rule of 40, market leadership |

**Investor-type adjustments:**
- **Angel** — speed, founder, story; lighter on financials, heavier on conviction and the "why you"
- **Accelerator / demo day** — 2–3 min, one killer metric, memorable hook, big vision; the video matters most
- **Seed fund** — thesis fit, insight, early traction, team; crisp deck + warm intro
- **Tier-1 VC partner meeting** — market size, defensibility, unit economics, the path to a fund-returning outcome; tight deck + data room
- **Strategic / corporate VC** — fit with their roadmap, partnership/distribution, less pure financial

**Deal-killers to pre-empt** (address before they ask): small market, no moat, bad unit economics, no traction at the stage's bar, weak/incomplete team, "vitamin not painkiller", crowded space with no wedge, founder can't articulate the ask.

Output: a one-paragraph **Targeting Brief** — audience, what they underwrite, the 3 things that win them, the 2 deal-killers to neutralize.

## SECTION 2 — NARRATIVE & MESSAGING ARCHITECTURE

The story is the product. Build it before the slides.

**Craft and output:**
- **One-liner** — `[Company] is [what] for [who] that [unique value].` One sentence, no jargon, memorable.
- **The hook (first 30 seconds)** — the opening that makes the investor lean in: a shocking stat, a sharp pain, a bold claim, or a "before/after" contrast. Never open with "we are a platform that…".
- **The core arc** — Problem (painful, urgent, big) → Why now (the inflection: tech/regulatory/behavioral shift) → Solution (the 10x, the "aha") → Why you (founder-market fit) → Where this goes (the vision/wedge-to-empire).
- **Positioning** — the category you own, the "vs status quo" contrast, the villain (the broken old way), the customer-as-hero frame.
- **Proof spine** — the 3–5 hardest facts that make the story undeniable (the growth number, the retention curve, the marquee logo, the founder credential, the unfair insight).
- **Message hierarchy** — primary message (the one thing they must remember), 3 supporting pillars, the proof under each.

Output: **Messaging Architecture** block — one-liner, hook, arc (5 beats), positioning, proof spine. This drives every asset below; do not duplicate it per asset — reference it.

## SECTION 3 — PITCH DECK (PPT)

Deliver the deck **slide-by-slide**. For each slide: the headline (the assertion, not a label), the body content/bullets, the data-viz or visual, and a one-line **speaker note** (what the founder says).

**Canonical slide order** (cut/merge to fit stage and time; mark optional slides):

1. **Cover** — logo, company, one-liner, the raise (`Raising $X seed`), contact. The 3-second first impression.
2. **Problem** — who hurts, how much, why it's urgent and big. Make them feel it.
3. **Solution** — your product, the "aha", the 10x. One sentence + one visual.
4. **Why now** — the market inflection that makes this inevitable today, not 5 years ago.
5. **Product** — how it works in 3 steps; screenshots/demo; the magic moment.
6. **Market size** — TAM / SAM / SOM, **bottom-up** (`# customers × ACV`), not a McKinsey top-down circle. Credible and big.
7. **Traction** — *the slide that raises the round.* Revenue/ARR, growth curve (up-and-to-the-right), retention/cohorts, logos, LOIs. If thin, show the strongest leading indicator. (Position earlier for Series A+.)
8. **Business model** — how you make money, pricing, ACV, gross margin, the unit economics teaser.
9. **Go-to-market** — how you acquire customers repeatably; CAC, LTV, payback, channels that work.
10. **Competition** — honest landscape; your wedge and moat (2×2 or feature matrix; never "we have no competitors").
11. **Team** — why *this* team wins; founder-market fit; key hires; notable advisors/investors.
12. **Financials** — 3-year projection, key metrics, path to the next milestone (top-down sanity, bottom-up build).
13. **The Ask** — amount, use of funds (3–4 buckets), the milestones it buys, runway, the vision close. End on ambition.
- **Appendix** (data room): detailed financials, cohort tables, pipeline, cap table, product roadmap, GTM detail, references.

**Design direction (the 10/20/30 + modern-deck rules):**
- One idea per slide; the **headline is the takeaway**, not a noun label ("Revenue grew 18% MoM for 9 months", not "Traction")
- Data-viz over text; big numbers big; ≤30–40 words/slide; ≥24pt body, ≥30pt is safer
- Consistent brand system (2–3 colors, 1–2 fonts, grid); high contrast; generous whitespace
- One chart per slide, labeled, with the takeaway annotated on the chart
- Two-deck reality: a sparse **read-aloud** deck (live) and a self-explanatory **send-ahead** deck (more text in speaker notes / leave-behind)

Output: numbered slide table + per-slide detail. Provide **speaker notes** for every slide.

| # | Slide | Headline (takeaway) | Visual / data-viz | Speaker note (say this) |
|---|-------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------------|

## SECTION 4 — VIDEO PRESENTATION

Produce a complete **video production package** (Claude does not render video — deliver everything a founder or editor needs to shoot/assemble it, and use design tools for visuals/screens).

**Pick the format:**
- **Demo-day / sizzle** — 60–120s, high energy, one killer metric, big vision, music-driven
- **Founder pitch video** — 90–180s, founder to camera, story-led, builds trust
- **Product demo** — 60–180s, screen-capture led, shows the magic moment working
- **Async send-ahead (Loom-style)** — 3–5 min, deck + talking head, for cold/warm outreach

**Deliverables:**
1. **Script** — timestamped, scene-by-scene, written for the ear (short sentences, spoken cadence). Structure: Hook (0–10s) → Problem (10–30s) → Solution/Demo (30–75s) → Traction/Proof (75–105s) → Team (105–120s) → Ask/CTA (120–end).
2. **Storyboard / shot list** — per scene: visual (talking head / screen-capture / B-roll / motion-graphic / text card), on-screen text, the spoken VO line, duration, transition.
3. **B-roll & asset list** — screen recordings to capture, stock clips, product shots, logos, charts to animate.
4. **Voice-over** — the VO script clean (record founder, or AI voice); tone and pace direction.
5. **Captions** — burned-in caption text (most investors watch muted first); style direction.
6. **Music & sound** — track mood/BPM, where it swells, sound-design beats.
7. **Spec** — aspect ratio + length per platform (1:1 or 9:16 for social teaser, 16:9 for demo day / email), resolution, where the CTA/contact card lands.

Output: format choice + 1-line rationale, the timestamped script, the storyboard/shot-list table, asset list, caption track, music/spec notes.

## SECTION 5 — ONE-PAGER / TEASER (PDF)

The skimmable, email-forwardable asset that earns the meeting. One page (sometimes two). Skimmable in 30 seconds.

**Content blocks (top to bottom):**
- Header — logo, one-liner, the raise + stage, contact
- Problem (1–2 lines) → Solution (1–2 lines)
- Market (the big credible number) | Traction (the 2–3 hardest metrics) — side by side
- Business model (one line: how you make money + margin)
- Why now + moat (one line each)
- Team (founders + 1-line credibility each)
- The Ask (amount, use of funds in one line, milestone it buys)
- Footer — website, email, deck/data-room link

**Layout direction:** two-column or banded layout, brand colors, 1–3 visuals max (logo, one chart, one product shot), high whitespace, scannable headers, big numbers. Designed to be read on a phone in an inbox.

Output: the one-pager content (every block filled) + a layout spec (grid, columns, where each block and visual sits).
